## 🎯 Google Staff Engineer — What They're Actually Looking For

### The 4 Things Google Evaluates at Staff Level

**1. Technical Excellence**
Not just knowing things — *demonstrating judgment*. "We could do X or Y. Here's why I chose X, here's what we'd need to be true for Y to be better."

**2. Leadership Without Authority**
Can you align 3 teams who don't report to you? Your Walmart story is perfect for this. Make sure you can tell it in 2 minutes.

**3. Problem Scoping**
Staff Engineers are expected to *define* the problem, not just solve it. Practice saying: "Before I jump to solutions, let me make sure I understand the constraints."

**4. Communication at Multiple Levels**
Can you explain your architecture to a junior engineer AND to a VP in the same conversation? This is a real interview signal.

## 💪 Confidence Plan — Because This Part Matters Too

**The impostor syndrome trap:** You have 11 years of real impact. But imposter syndrome will make you doubt every answer in an interview. Here's how to combat it:

1. **Write your wins down.** Every Sunday, spend 10 minutes writing 3 things you did that week that a junior engineer couldn't have done. Read it before interviews.

2. **Do 1 mock interview per month** (starting month 2). Use Interviewing.io or ask a peer. The fear of interviews comes from unfamiliarity, not incompetence.

3. **LinkedIn is your confidence builder.** Every post that gets 50+ likes is evidence that smart people value your thinking. Collect that evidence.

4. **Talk to people at Google.** Find 2–3 Staff Engineers on LinkedIn (not asking for referrals yet, just coffee chats). Understanding their day-to-day will make the role feel real and achievable.

5. **Don't compare your chapter 1 to someone else's chapter 10.** Your resume already has Staff-level impact. You're not building from zero — you're making what you've built *legible* to interviewers.
